Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun in our solar system." It is approximately half the size of Earth, with a diameter of about 6,792 kilometers (4,220 miles). Mars is located at an average distance of about 225 million kilometers (140 million miles) from the Sun.

Jupiter

Jupiter has a diameter of about 143,000 kilometers (88,800 miles), making it more than 11 times larger than Earth. Its massive size allows it to hold approximately 1,300 Earths within its volume. In terms of mass, Jupiter is about 2.5 times more massive than all the other planets in the solar system combined.

Neptune

Neptune's atmosphere is known for its dynamic weather patterns, including strong winds and the presence of storms. One notable storm is the Great Dark Spot, which is similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot.

Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and is located about 384,400 kilometers (238,900 miles) away from Earth. It is the fifth-largest moon in the solar system and has a diameter of about 3,474 kilometers (2,159 miles), which is roughly one-fourth the size of Earth.
